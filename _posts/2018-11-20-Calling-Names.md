Everyone knows the internet was designed as a new way to call names.

Those names just don't always keep their values, and that's why the various names and URLs for this site and blog have been morphing-around in recent months.

Botzilla.com's original name came from a service that wrote chatbots and chat-assistant bots. Since then, several other people have decided that they should use the same name for, well, robots in league destruction derbies, for their instagram accounts, for their reddit threads, and many other things. Every couple of months someone writes to me about their great idea that they want to name "botzilla" and so won't I hand them they domain name (never: "will you sell the domain name? I have significant funds..."). The latest such email came from a fellow who had the great idea of using it to describe his service for chatbots.

When I added a blog to Botzilla, mainly concerned with the nascent world of internet photography, I called it PhotoRant and dutifully registered photorant.com to aim, well, back at botzilla. Since then there have been at least a half-dozen other sites using variations of the name so I dropped the $40/year fee and now the DNS belongs to some registrar in Arizona who appears to be camping on the name. Or maybe he's one of the not-remotely-me Instagram accounts or flickr accounts or tumblr accounts.

Even my personal name hasn't been immune: when I went to register kevinbjorke.com I first found it was registered to a camper in Dubai who wanted "six figures" for me to use my own name. Since then I've seen the title transfer to a mysterious corporation in Japan. At least they forgot to register [www.kevin-bjorke.com] which does aim to Botzilla, along with [bjorke.photo] and [bjorke.photography] which might just as easily aim at Rebecca Bjorke's  [The Bekkaffect](http://www.thebekkaffect.com/) -- probably what you  came here looking for anyway.
